## AI & Machine Learning

### Google's TurboQuant Breakthrough Pressures Memory Chip Stocks
- **Source:** CNBC, The Verge, Google Research
- **URL:** https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/google-ai-turboquant-memory-chip-stocks-samsung-micron.html
- **Summary:** Google unveiled TurboQuant, a compression method that can reduce AI model memory usage by six times with zero accuracy loss. The announcement triggered a sell-off in memory stocks — SK Hynix fell 6%, Samsung nearly 5%, and Micron dropped in pre-market. Cloudflare's CEO called it "Google's DeepSeek." However, SemiAnalysis argues improved efficiency will ultimately drive more capable models that need even more hardware, not less.

### Anthropic Security Lapse Reveals Unreleased "Mythos" Model
- **Source:** Fortune, The Verge
- **URL:** https://fortune.com/2026/03/26/anthropic-leaked-unreleased-model-exclusive-event-security-issues-cybersecurity-unsecured-data-store/

The data store was publicly accessible without login. Anthropic attributed the lapse to "human error in the CMS configuration" and secured it after Fortune reported the issue. The company stressed AI coding agents were not at fault.

### Bluesky Launches "Attie" — An AI App for Custom Social Feeds
- **Source:** Engadget, TechCrunch, The Verge
- **URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/29/bluesky-leans-into-ai-with-attie-an-app-for-building-custom-feeds/

Bluesky says Attie will eventually be able to "vibe code" entire apps for the decentralized protocol, signaling a major product expansion beyond its core social network.

### Apple to Open Siri to Third-Party AI Extensions in iOS 27
- **Source:** Bloomberg (Mark Gurman), The Verge
- **URL:** https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence
- **Summary:** Apple is planning to open Siri and Apple Intelligence to third-party AI services via a new "Extensions" feature in iOS 27. Extensions will have a dedicated App Store section, effectively creating an AI App Store. Users will be able to install third-party chatbots beyond ChatGPT and run them inside Siri. The move follows Apple's deal with Google to use Gemini for distilling smaller on-device models.

## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **War Risk Repricing:** The Iran conflict has entered a potentially new phase with U.S. ground troops, Houthi involvement, and attacks on Gulf infrastructure. Markets are repricing everything — oil, equities, shipping, inflation expectations — with no clear resolution in sight.

2. **Oil as the New Macro Variable:** Brent above $115 and WTI above $100 are reshaping the global economic outlook. If the Strait of Hormuz remains constrained past mid-April, expect recession fears to intensify. Central banks face an impossible trilemma of war-driven inflation, slowing growth, and market instability.

3. **AI Efficiency vs. AI Infrastructure Spending:** Google's TurboQuant suggests models can do more with less memory, but the capex bubble in AI infrastructure spending continues to grow. Watch for a potential "Jevons paradox" — efficiency gains leading to even more compute demand.

4. **Cyber-Kinetic Convergence:** Iran's synchronized physical strikes and cyber campaigns represent a new warfare paradigm. Nearly 5,800 cyberattacks in a month signal that cyber is now a core component of conventional conflict, not a sideshow.

5. **SaaSpocalypse Creates Winners and Losers:** Traditional SaaS incumbents are getting hammered while AI-native companies attract massive capital. OpenAI's $120B+ raise at $730B valuation stands in stark contrast to Salesforce and ServiceNow losing 30% of their value.

6. **Defense Tech's Moment:** The cost asymmetry of modern warfare ($2M missile vs. $35K drone) is finally catalyzing the defense tech sector. Companies with proven battlefield technology — Anduril, SpektreWorks, AeroVironment — stand to benefit from a fundamental shift in Pentagon procurement.
