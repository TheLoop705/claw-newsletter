Why electrical I/O hits a wall in 2026-27, and who owns the photons that replace it

Executive Summary & Key Takeaways

One-line thesis: Electrical I/O has hit a wall — the next 5 years of AI scale-out runs on photons. Own the BOM (lasers, foundries, DSP, packaging) before CPO ships in volume in H2'26.

1. Why now — the thermodynamic wall

The story of compute since 1965 has been Moore's Law on transistors. The story of compute from 2025 onward is bandwidth, not gates. A modern AI training cluster is bottlenecked not by FLOPs but by how fast you can shuffle weights, activations, and gradients between accelerators. NVIDIA's GB200 NVL72 already burns ~120 kW per rack — and roughly 30-40% of that power is electrical I/O and SerDes, not compute.

Three independent walls are converging:

  1. SerDes signal integrity. 224 Gbps PAM4 over copper is at the practical limit (~1m reach, ~10 dB loss budget). 448 Gbps electrical is signal-integrity hostile — you need either DSP-heavy retimers (AECs) or you go optical.
  2. Power per bit. Pluggable optics (~15 pJ/bit at 800G) → CPO (~3-5 pJ/bit) → photonic compute (~0.1 pJ/bit). At 1M-GPU scale fabrics, this is the difference between a 200 MW and a 600 MW datacenter.
  3. Reach. Copper dies at ~1m at 224G. AI clusters that scale beyond a single rack need fiber — and once you're on fiber, the laser/modulator BOM owns the economics.

8. Bear case — what kills this

I've spent most of this issue describing the bull. Here's how it ends in tears:

  1. CPO yield wall. Hermetic photonic packaging at >70% yield is unproven at volume. If TSMC COUPE ramps slow (yields stuck at 50%), the entire 2026-27 catalyst calendar slides 12-18 months. Premium multiples on COHR/LITE/FN compress hard.
  2. LPO/LRO eats the TAM. Linear-drive pluggables (LPO) at 1.6T are 30% cheaper than DSP-based modules and "good enough" for most fabrics. If LPO becomes the standard, MRVL/AVGO DSP TAM gets cut, and CPO's value prop weakens.
  3. AI capex air-pocket. Any DeepSeek-style efficiency shock or hyperscaler capex pause hits the entire optics complex disproportionately — these are torque names.
  4. Geopolitics. ~70% of optical module assembly today happens in China (Innolight, HiSense, Eoptolink). Tariff escalation reshuffles the BOM but creates 12-month transition pain.

Risk-managed sizing: keep the photonics sleeve <25% of total equity, and split the basket across foundry / DSP / laser / packaging so no single layer failure unwinds the whole thesis.

9. The under-discussed angle

Everyone talks about transceiver ASPs and CPO timelines. Almost nobody talks about lasers as the actual choke point. A single Quantum-X CPO module needs ~16 external DFB lasers. A hyperscaler ordering 100K modules needs 1.6M lasers. Coherent + Lumentum's combined InP DFB capacity in 2026 is roughly 8M units/year — comfortable today, uncomfortable in 2027 if NVDA, AMD, Broadcom, and Marvell all ship CPO simultaneously. This is why I weight COHR > MRVL on a 24-month view despite MRVL being the higher-beta narrative — the constraint, not the demand, prices the equity.

The second-order play here is quantum-dot lasers (more thermally stable, longer life). Whoever scales QD first re-prices the whole laser BOM. Watch privates Aurrion (Juniper-acquired), Quintessent, and university IP coming out of UCSB — that's where the next M&A target will emerge.

This issue is research, not advice. I hold positions in NVDA, AVGO, TSM, COHR. Past performance, future performance, you know the drill.

Key Takeaways

  • 1. Copper SerDes is hitting a thermodynamic wall at 224G/lane — 448G is signal-integrity hostile, forcing optical I/O onto the package by 2026-27 in AI scale-out fabrics
  • 2. CPO TAM inflects from <$0.5B (2025) to ~$5-7B (2027) and ~$25B (2030) per LightCounting / Dell'Oro — a ~30%+ CAGR concentrated in 4-5 vendors
  • 3. The bottleneck is NOT silicon — it is external laser sources (DFB / quantum-dot), high-bandwidth modulators (thin-film LiNbO3), and hermetic photonic packaging at >70% yield
  • 4. NVIDIA Quantum-X CPO switches enter volume in H2'26; this is the single biggest dated catalyst — Coherent, Lumentum, Fabrinet, MRVL all sit in that BOM
  • 5. Pure-play photonic compute (Lightmatter, Ayar Labs) remains private; the listed proxy basket — POET, LWLG, AEHR, CRDO — is high beta and high binary risk, size accordingly
  • 6. Bear case: CPO slips to 2028 (yield/reliability), pluggable 1.6T LPO/LRO eats the near-term TAM, and incumbents (AVGO, MRVL) capture all the value while pure-plays burn cash
