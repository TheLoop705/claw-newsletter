The Nuclear Energy Renaissance

AI data centers are reigniting nuclear power demand. From uranium miners to next-gen reactors, here's how to position.

Executive Summary & Macro Thesis

Nuclear energy is experiencing a generational renaissance driven by a single, overwhelming force: AI data centers need baseload power, and nuclear is the only carbon-free source that can deliver it at scale, 24/7. Hyperscalers have moved from exploration to execution — Microsoft signed a 20-year PPA with Constellation Energy to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1, Amazon acquired the Talen Energy data center campus adjacent to the Susquehanna nuclear plant, and Google signed a deal with Kairos Power for small modular reactors. This is not speculation. This is capital being deployed.

The Macro Case

AI power demand: US data center electricity consumption is projected to double from ~4% of total grid demand to 8-12% by 2030. Each new hyperscale data center requires 100-1,000 MW of continuous baseload power. Renewables alone cannot meet this — intermittency, land use, and grid connection constraints make nuclear the logical complement. Microsoft, Amazon, Google, and Meta have all publicly stated nuclear is part of their energy strategy.

Uranium supply/demand: The uranium market has been in structural deficit since 2019. Primary mine supply covers only ~75% of reactor demand. Years of underinvestment after the Fukushima-driven price crash (2011-2020) hollowed out the supply base. New mine development takes 10-15 years. Spot uranium has risen from ~$20/lb in 2020 to over $100/lb, and the supply gap is widening as 60+ new reactors are under construction globally.

Regulatory tailwinds: The NRC approved NuScale's SMR design certification in 2023 — the first-ever for a small modular reactor. Bipartisan support for nuclear in the US is at multi-decade highs with the ADVANCE Act streamlining licensing. Globally, 30+ countries pledged to triple nuclear capacity by 2050 at COP28. China is building reactors at a pace of 6-8 per year.

Cycle duration: This is a 10-20 year structural cycle, not a trade. The buildout of AI infrastructure is a multi-decade trend. New reactor construction takes 5-10 years. Uranium supply response is equally slow. The companies positioned today will compound for years.

Key Takeaways

  • 1. AI data center power demand is the primary catalyst — hyperscaler PPAs are validating nuclear as essential infrastructure
  • 2. Uranium is in structural deficit with no quick supply fix — Cameco is the premier way to play this
  • 3. Constellation Energy operates the largest US nuclear fleet and is the direct beneficiary of hyperscaler deals
  • 4. Next-gen reactors (SMR, OKLO) offer asymmetric upside but carry substantial execution and timeline risk
  • 5. This is a 10-20 year structural cycle — position for duration, not momentum
