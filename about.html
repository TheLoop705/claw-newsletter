About

Investment research on physical infrastructure businesses with software-like valuations.

The Thesis

The "atoms over bits" investment thesis posits that physical infrastructure businesses with irreplaceable moats are commanding software-like valuations. As AI drives demand for compute, energy, and advanced manufacturing, the winners are those who own the integration layer — software control with proprietary hardware advantages.

What We Cover

Semiconductor Infrastructure

Equipment, materials, and manufacturing processes that enable advanced chip production. From EUV lithography to process control.

Energy & Power

Data center power infrastructure, grid modernization, and next-generation energy solutions including nuclear and renewables.

Robotics & Automation

Industrial automation, warehouse robotics, and the software layer that orchestrates physical operations at scale.

Advanced Materials

Silicon carbide, rare earths, battery materials, and other critical inputs for next-generation technology manufacturing.

Methodology

Each issue is generated via parallel sub-agent research using OpenClaw. Multiple AI agents investigate themes and companies simultaneously, then synthesize findings into actionable investment briefings.

Our research process combines quantitative analysis (financial metrics, valuation multiples, market positioning) with qualitative assessment (competitive moats, management quality, geopolitical risks). Every recommendation includes clear bull and bear cases, price targets, and position sizing guidance.
