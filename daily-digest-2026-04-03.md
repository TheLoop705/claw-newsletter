## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **Microsoft Goes Independent on AI:** The launch of three in-house foundational models and Suleyman's superintelligence pivot mark a structural shift. Microsoft is no longer just an OpenAI reseller — it's building its own frontier capabilities. Watch for competitive dynamics to intensify across the AI value chain.

2. **Iran War = Energy Shock 2.0:** With €14B added to EU energy imports and the Strait of Hormuz under threat, the energy sector is rallying (XLE at 100% technical breadth). OPEC+'s Sunday meeting and any ceasefire developments are the key near-term catalysts.

3. **Record VC Funding Masks Concentration Risk:** $300B in Q1 is staggering, but most of it went to a handful of AI mega-rounds. Early-stage non-AI startups may face a capital squeeze as LPs concentrate bets on proven AI winners.

4. **Semiconductor Geopolitics Intensify:** The MATCH Act, Chinese chipmakers claiming 41% market share, Nvidia's $2B Marvell deal, and Intel's 18A milestone all point to an accelerating bifurcation of the global chip ecosystem. The battle lines are hardening.

5. **Food M&A Goes Mega:** The $65B Unilever-McCormick merger signals Big Food is consolidating aggressively, seeking scale and synergies in an inflationary environment. Expect more CPG mega-deals this year.

6. **AI Agents Create New Attack Surface:** RSAC 2026's dominant theme — agentic AI security — is becoming investable. Companies securing AI agent workflows (identity, permissions, audit trails) represent the next cybersecurity growth vector.
