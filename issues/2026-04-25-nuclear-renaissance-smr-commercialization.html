Nuclear Renaissance — The SMR Commercialization Trade

From hyperscaler PPAs to first commercial FIDs: who owns the bottlenecks

Executive Summary & Key Takeaways

Why now

Three things converged in Q1 2026 that didn't exist 18 months ago.

First, commercial SMR FIDs are landing. TVA's Clinch River (GE-Hitachi BWRX-300) is targeting FID this year. Dow/X-energy's Seadrift Xe-100 is advancing through NRC. OPG Darlington pours start in 2026. This is the moment "SMR" stops being a slide deck and becomes a capex line item.

Second, hyperscaler PPAs have repriced the existing fleet. Microsoft–Constellation's Three Mile Island restart deal ($100+/MWh, 20 years), Amazon–Talen Susquehanna, Google–Kairos, Meta–Constellation Clinton. Behind-the-meter nuclear is now the scarcest commodity in the AI buildout. CEG re-rated from a utility multiple (~14x) to ~25-28x fwd on this dynamic alone.

Third, the uranium structural deficit is real. Primary supply runs ~140Mlb against reactor demand of ~190Mlb. Secondary stocks are drawing down. Kazatomprom is guiding below plan. The Russian LEU Ban Act forced Western enrichment reshoring with no ramp plan in place. Spot bounced off $65 but term price is $80+ and rising.

Layer on the bipartisan ADVANCE Act (signed July 2024), DOE's $900M SMR funding, and 45V Clean Hydrogen credits and you have rare clean policy alignment.

The asymmetry

The market is pricing operators (CEG, VST) as utilities-with-AI-optionality and developers (OKLO, SMR, NNE) as venture bets. Both framings under-discount the bottleneck: there are ~94 operating reactors in the US, and the queue to add ANY new megawatt — restart, uprate, or SMR — runs through a tiny set of chokepoints: NRC licensing, large-forging supply chain, HALEU fuel, EPC labor. Whoever owns those chokepoints earns supernormal rents for a decade.

What's missing from the consensus take

Most write-ups stop at "AI needs power → buy nuclear." The under-discussed angle: the fuel cycle is the real tightest link. You can announce an SMR. You cannot announce HALEU into existence. Centrus, Urenco, and Orano are the only Western enrichers, and ramping enrichment capacity takes 5+ years from greenfield.

This is why the trade construction below puts CCJ alongside CEG as a core position rather than treating uranium as a satellite.

Bull case (5-year)

  • ~50GW incremental US load by 2030 from data centers alone (EPRI/LBNL). Nuclear is the only firm clean baseload that scales to that demand.
  • Tail risk is upside. First successful FOAK SMR commissioning re-rates the entire developer cohort. Uranium spot squeezes on any supply shock.
  • Sovereign demand. UK, Poland, Czechia, Romania, KSA, UAE all signing. Western fleet builders are capacity-constrained.

Bear case

  • FOAK cost overruns. Vogtle 3/4 ran $35B over budget. SMR developers claim modularity is different — true in theory, unproven until two units deliver on schedule.
  • Uranium can correct hard. Sprott Trust dynamics and speculative positioning mean -25% drawdowns are normal even in a bull market. Position sizing matters more here than in most themes.
  • Multiple compression on operators if the AI capex narrative breaks (DeepSeek-style efficiency shock, hyperscaler capex cuts).
  • Political risk. Bipartisan now, but a single high-profile incident resets sentiment for years.

Trade construction

Core (60%): CEG + CCJ — both have real cash flows and torque to the theme. Satellite (25%): GEV + LEU + BWXT — adjacent bottlenecks (turbines, HALEU, naval reactors). Lottery (15%): OKLO and/or URNM basket — pure option value, sized to survive a -50% drawdown.

Catalysts to watch (next 6 months)

  • TVA Clinch River FID (expected H2 2026)
  • Next hyperscaler PPA announcements (likely candidates: AMZN, META capacity expansions)
  • Q2 uranium term contracting round (utilities running thin on coverage post-2028)
  • NRC Part 53 rulemaking finalization
  • DOE HALEU allocation decisions

Bottom line

Nuclear is no longer a contrarian trade — but the consensus version of the trade still misses the chokepoints. Own the operators with PPA torque (CEG, VST). Own the fuel cycle (CCJ, LEU). Treat developers as call options. And remember: in this theme, you cannot reverse-engineer HALEU.
