## AI & Machine Learning

### OpenAI Working to Merge ChatGPT, Codex, and Atlas Into One App
- **Source:** The Verge
- **URL:** https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence
- **Summary:** OpenAI is working on consolidating its suite of products — ChatGPT, the Codex coding assistant, and the Atlas web browser — into a single unified application. The move signals OpenAI's ambition to create an all-in-one AI workspace rather than maintaining fragmented products. This follows a broader industry trend where AI companies are bundling capabilities into single interfaces to increase user retention and daily engagement.

### Signal Creator Moxie Marlinspike Brings End-to-End Encryption to Meta AI
- **Source:** Confer Blog, The Verge
- **URL:** https://confer.to/blog/2026/03/encrypted-meta/
- **Summary:** Moxie Marlinspike, creator of the Signal Protocol, announced that his encrypted AI chatbot startup Confer will integrate its privacy technology to underpin Meta AI. The deal echoes Marlinspike's landmark 2014 work integrating end-to-end encryption into WhatsApp for billions. Confer will remain an independent entity while providing the cryptographic foundation for Meta's AI products. Marlinspike wrote that AI chat apps have become "some of the largest centralized data lakes in history" and called the current lack of encryption unacceptable.

### Microsoft Launches MAI-Image-2, Cracks Top 3 on Arena.ai
- **Source:** Microsoft AI
- **URL:** https://microsoft.ai/news/introducing-MAI-Image-2/
- **Summary:** Microsoft's AI Superintelligence (MAI) team released MAI-Image-2, a second-generation text-to-image model now ranked #3 on the Arena.ai leaderboard. The model features enhanced photorealism, reliable in-image text generation, and rich scene generation. It's rolling out in Copilot and Bing Image Creator, with API access available to select enterprise customers. Microsoft noted its next-generation GB200 compute cluster is now operational.

### Meta AI to Replace Human Content Moderators Over Coming Years
- **Source:** Meta, The Verge
- **URL:** https://about.fb.com/news/2026/03/boosting-your-support-and-safety-on-metas-apps-with-ai/
- **Summary:** Meta announced a global rollout of its AI support assistant for Facebook and Instagram, stating it will "reduce reliance on third-party vendors" for content enforcement. The AI assistant handles account support, scam reporting, privacy settings, and content appeals — responding in under five seconds. Meta said AI moderation will take over tasks like "repetitive reviews of graphic content" and combating scam and drug sales patterns, while humans remain for oversight. The move comes as content moderators have increasingly organized for better working conditions.

## Tech Policy & Regulation

### Pentagon Rebuts Anthropic's Lawsuit Over "Supply Chain Risk" Designation
- **Source:** The Verge
- **URL:** https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence
- **Summary:** The U.S. Department of Defense filed a rebuttal to Anthropic's lawsuit challenging its "supply chain risk" designation. ## Cybersecurity

### FBI Seizes Handala Hacktivist Sites After Stryker Attack Wiped 80,000 Devices
- **Source:** BleepingComputer
- **URL:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fbi-seizes-handala-data-leak-site-after-stryker-cyberattack/
- **Summary:** The FBI seized two websites belonging to Handala, an Iranian-linked hacktivist group, following a devastating cyberattack on medical technology giant Stryker. The attackers compromised a Windows domain admin account and used Microsoft Intune's remote "wipe" command to factory reset approximately 80,000 devices, including employees' personal devices. CISA subsequently issued guidance on hardening Intune configurations. Handala acknowledged the seizures and vowed to continue operations from new infrastructure.

### Critical 'PolyShell' Flaw Enables Unauthenticated RCE on All Magento Stores
- **Source:** BleepingComputer
- **URL:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-polyshell-flaw-allows-unauthenticated-rce-on-magento-e-stores/
- **Summary:** A newly disclosed vulnerability dubbed "PolyShell" affects all Magento Open Source and Adobe Commerce version 2 installations, allowing unauthenticated remote code execution and account takeover. The critical flaw poses immediate risk to the hundreds of thousands of e-commerce sites running on the platform. Patches and mitigations are expected from Adobe.
