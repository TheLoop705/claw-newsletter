## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **Iran War Reshapes Markets:** The U.S.-Israel military campaign against Iran is the dominant macro force. Oil at ~$98–103/barrel, record strategic reserve releases, and Strait of Hormuz disruption are creating the largest supply shock in oil market history. Energy stocks benefit; everything else faces headwinds.

2. **AI Infrastructure Hits an Inflection:** Morgan Stanley's report, NVIDIA's GTC, and GPT-5.4's release converge to signal that AI capabilities are accelerating faster than most investors expected. The 9–18 GW power shortfall through 2028 makes energy-adjacent plays (Constellation, nuclear, gas turbines) critical.

3. **State-Level AI Regulation Explodes:** With 78 bills across 27 states, AI regulation is happening at the state level regardless of federal action. The FTC's new AI preemption framework could clash with this wave, creating legal uncertainty for AI companies.

4. **Cyber War Goes Corporate:** The Stryker attack shows Iranian-linked hackers are targeting U.S. companies as the kinetic war expands. Healthcare, defense contractors, and critical infrastructure are at elevated risk. Cybersecurity stocks and services providers should see increased demand.

5. **Semiconductor Supply Chain at Risk:** While TSMC dominance (70% market share) and chip sales growth ($1T target) look strong, the Iran war introduces material risks to chipmaking supplies and energy costs. The BESI takeover interest highlights how advanced packaging is becoming strategically critical.

6. **Funding Frenzy Continues:** $189B in global startup funding in February alone, VAST Data at $30B, and $225B raised in U.S. equity rounds YTD — capital deployment into AI infrastructure shows no signs of slowing despite market volatility.
