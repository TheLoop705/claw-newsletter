## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **Nvidia Delivers, But the AI Trade Broadens:** Nvidia's $68.1B quarter confirms AI infrastructure demand is real, but AMD's mega-deal with Meta and DeepSeek's snub of US chipmakers signal the competitive landscape is diversifying. The AI chip duopoly is evolving into a multipolar contest.

2. **Pentagon vs. AI Labs:** The Anthropic blacklisting threat represents a new front in AI governance — not regulation, but direct government leverage over AI companies' military participation. This has implications for every frontier AI lab.

3. **Capital Paradox in AI:** Record funding rounds ($30B Anthropic, $100B+ OpenAI) coexist with historically thin discretionary capital for M&A. Money is concentrated, not abundant — the gap between AI haves and have-nots widens.

4. **China's Silicon Independence Accelerates:** DeepSeek favoring Huawei over Nvidia, China's 5x chip output ramp, and domestic HBM3 production all point to an accelerating decoupling of the global semiconductor ecosystem.

5. **Energy Infrastructure Is the AI Bottleneck:** $600B in planned spending vs. grid reality. Coal mandates from the Trump administration and utility risk-sharing concerns suggest the energy-AI nexus will be 2026's defining infrastructure challenge.
