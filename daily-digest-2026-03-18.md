## Cybersecurity

### GlassWorm Supply-Chain Attack Hits 400+ Repos on GitHub, npm, VSCode
- **Source:** BleepingComputer, Aikido, Socket, Step Security
- **URL:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/glassworm-malware-hits-400-plus-code-repos-on-github-npm-vscode-openvsx/
- **Summary:** The GlassWorm supply-chain campaign returned with its most extensive wave yet, compromising 433 packages, repositories, and extensions across GitHub, npm, and VSCode/OpenVSX. Researchers linked the campaign to a single threat actor via a shared Solana blockchain C2 address. The malware uses Unicode character obfuscation to hide credential-harvesting and crypto-wallet theft code.

### New Font-Rendering Attack Poisons AI Assistants
- **Source:** BleepingComputer, LayerX
- **URL:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-font-rendering-trick-hides-malicious-commands-from-ai-tools/
- **Summary:** Security researchers at LayerX demonstrated a novel attack using custom fonts and CSS to display malicious commands to users while hiding them from AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Gemini, and others). The attack exploits the disconnect between how browsers render pages visually and how AI tools parse the underlying HTML. Successfully tested against 12+ AI assistants as of December 2025.

### Apple Pushes First "Background Security Improvements" Update
- **Source:** BleepingComputer
- **URL:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/apple-pushes-first-background-security-improvements-update-to-fix-webkit-flaw/
- **Summary:** Apple released its first-ever Background Security Improvements update to fix a WebKit vulnerability (CVE-2026-20643) on iPhones, iPads, and Macs — without requiring a full OS upgrade. This represents a new, lighter-weight patching mechanism for critical security fixes.

## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **Iran War Dominates Everything:** The escalating Iran conflict is the primary driver across all asset classes. With Hormuz shipping at ~20% of pre-war levels and retaliatory strikes intensifying, the risk premium isn't going away. Citi's $130–$200 oil scenarios deserve attention.

2. **Fed in a Bind:** The Fed holds rates today, but the real question is how Powell frames oil-driven inflation risk. If he signals concern about energy costs feeding into core inflation, rate cut expectations (already pushed to September) could evaporate entirely.

3. **Nvidia's China Restart is Big:** H200 orders from China re-entering the picture could add billions in previously written-off revenue. Watch for execution — the 25% government cut and licensing requirements add friction.

4. **Supply-Chain Security Escalation:** GlassWorm hitting 433+ repos across the developer ecosystem is a wake-up call. The convergence of AI-targeted attacks (font-rendering poison) and supply-chain compromises signals a new threat surface.

5. **AI Enterprise Shift:** Both Mistral (Forge) and OpenAI (IPO pivot to coding/enterprise) are moving toward enterprise monetization. The consumer AI gold rush may be peaking; B2B is where the revenue story is heading.

6. **Private Credit Stress:** Redemption pressures in private credit are worth watching as a canary for broader financial stress, especially if war-driven volatility persists.
