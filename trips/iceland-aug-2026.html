🗺️Day-by-Day Itinerary

South Coast focus — the most scenic bang-for-buck with 5 days. Full Ring Road needs 7–10 days.

01
Arrival & Reykjavik
Monday, August 24
  • 🛬 Land at Keflavik (KEF) — pick up rental car at airport
  • 🛒 Stop at duty-free for alcohol, then Bónus supermarket for road trip snacks & groceries
  • ♨️ Sky Lagoon (more local vibe) or Blue Lagoon (iconic, book ahead!) — perfect jet-lag cure
  • 🌃 Explore Reykjavik: Hallgrímskirkja, Laugavegur street, harbour area
  • 🍺 Dinner & drinks downtown — check out Skúli Craft Bar or Mikkeller
🚗 KEF → Reykjavik: 45 min
02
Golden Circle
Tuesday, August 25
  • 🌋 Þingvellir National Park — UNESCO site, walk between tectonic plates
  • 🤿 Silfra Snorkeling — crystal clear glacial water between continents (book ahead!)
  • 💨 Geysir Geothermal Area — watch Strokkur erupt every 5–10 min
  • 💧 Gullfoss Waterfall — massive two-tiered waterfall, one of Iceland's most powerful
  • ♨️ Secret Lagoon in Flúðir — natural hot spring, less touristy than Blue Lagoon
  • 🌙 Drive to Selfoss or Hella for overnight
🚗 Total: ~230 km loop, 3.5 hours driving
03
South Coast — Waterfalls & Black Sand
Wednesday, August 26
  • 💧 Seljalandsfoss — walk behind the waterfall (bring rain gear!)
  • 💧 Skógafoss — epic 60m waterfall, climb the stairs for top view
  • 🏖️ Reynisfjara Black Sand Beach — basalt columns, sea stacks, huge waves (stay back from the water!)
  • 🏘️ Explore Vík — charming village, lunch stop
  • 🌿 Fjaðrárgljúfur Canyon — stunning 100m deep moss-covered canyon
  • 🌙 Stay near Kirkjubæjarklaustur or Höfn
🚗 Selfoss → Vík → Kirkjubæjarklaustur: ~250 km, 3 hours
04
Glaciers & Ice Lagoon
Thursday, August 27
  • 🏔️ Skaftafell / Vatnajökull National Park — glacier hiking tour on Europe's largest glacier
  • 🧊 Jökulsárlón Glacier Lagoon — floating icebergs, zodiac boat tour
  • 💎 Diamond Beach — ice chunks washed up on black sand, incredible photos
  • 🐋 Optional: Whale watching from Höfn (humpback whales, dolphins)
  • 🌙 Stay in Höfn (lobster capital!) — treat yourselves to langoustine dinner
🚗 Kirkjubæjarklaustur → Jökulsárlón → Höfn: ~200 km, 2.5 hours
05
Return & Departure
Friday, August 29 (or Saturday Aug 29)
  • 🚗 Drive back to Reykjavik (long drive — leave early!)
  • 🛍️ Last-minute shopping: Laugavegur for souvenirs, Icelandic wool sweaters
  • 🍔 Bæjarins Beztu — famous Reykjavik hot dog stand (a must)
  • ♨️ Optional: quick dip at Reykjavik public pool (Vesturbæjarlaug)
  • 🛫 Drop off car, fly home to Frankfurt
🚗 Höfn → KEF Airport: ~460 km, 5.5 hours (start early!)
💡 Alternative: If the return drive feels too long, break Day 4 earlier and sleep near Vík on the way back. Or book a late flight.

🎯Must-Do Activities

🏔️ Glacier Hiking

Walk on Sólheimajökull or Skaftafell glacier with crampons. Guided only. 3–4 hour tours.

~€80–120 per person

🤿 Silfra Snorkeling

Snorkel between tectonic plates in crystal clear 2°C water. Dry suit provided. Bucket list stuff.

~€120–150 per person

🐋 Whale Watching

Humpback whales, minke whales, dolphins. Best from Húsavík or Reykjavik. August = peak season.

~€80–100 per person

♨️ Blue Lagoon / Sky Lagoon

Geothermal spa in milky blue water. Sky Lagoon is newer, less crowded, ocean views.

€60–90 per person

🧊 Jökulsárlón Boat Tour

Zodiac boat ride among floating icebergs. Get up close. ~1 hour tour.

~€45–65 per person

🌋 Volcano / Lava Field Hike

Recent eruptions on Reykjanes Peninsula. Guided tours to fresh lava fields.

~€60–100 per person

🐴 Icelandic Horse Riding

Ride the unique five-gaited Icelandic horse through lava fields. No experience needed.

~€70–100 per person

🌊 Secret Lagoon

Natural hot spring in Flúðir. Cheaper and more authentic than Blue Lagoon.

~€25 per person
🌌 Northern Lights? Late August is borderline — it starts getting dark enough around Aug 25–30. You might catch a faint display if you're lucky and far from city lights. Don't count on it, but keep an eye on the aurora forecast!

📋Practical Info

💳 Money & Payment

  • Currency: Icelandic Króna (ISK) — 1 EUR ≈ 150 ISK
  • Cards accepted literally everywhere — even hot dog stands and remote gas stations
  • No need to carry cash at all
  • Tipping is not expected in Iceland

🚗 Driving Tips

  • Speed limit: 90 km/h on highways, 80 on gravel, 50 in towns
  • Speed cameras everywhere — fines are brutal (€150+)
  • F-roads (highland roads) require a 4x4. Regular cars = insurance void
  • Fuel stations can be 100+ km apart — fill up when you can
  • Use N1 fuel card or credit card with PIN at unmanned stations
  • Single-lane bridges are common — first to arrive has right of way
  • Watch for sheep on roads! They roam free in summer
  • Wind: Open car doors carefully — wind can rip them off (yes, really)

📱 Apps & Connectivity

  • Vedur — official weather app (weather changes hourly)
  • Road.is (Vegagerðin) — road conditions, closures, webcams
  • Google Maps works great — download Iceland offline map before going
  • Mobile data: Buy a Siminn or Vodafone tourist SIM at KEF airport (~€20 for 10GB)
  • Good 4G coverage on Ring Road, patchy in highlands

🍺 Alcohol in Iceland

  • Alcohol is only sold at Vínbúðin (state liquor stores) — not in supermarkets
  • Vínbúðin has limited hours (closed Sundays in many places)
  • BUY AT DUTY FREE ON ARRIVAL AT KEF — this is not optional, it's survival
  • A beer at a bar: €8–12. A bottle of wine at Vínbúðin: €15–25

🌤️ August Weather

  • Temperature: 10–15°C (can feel colder with wind)
  • Daylight: ~17–19 hours (sunrise ~5 AM, sunset ~9:30 PM)
  • Rain is frequent but usually light — expect all four seasons in one day
  • Late August: starting to get dark at night → slight chance of northern lights!

🚙 Car Rental

  • Book a 4x4 SUV if you want to explore F-roads or feel safe on gravel
  • A regular car is fine if sticking to Ring Road (Route 1)
  • Best companies: Lotus, Blue Car, Hertz Iceland
  • Get gravel protection + sand/ash protection insurance — worth it
  • Estimated cost: €300–550 for 5 days (4x4 SUV, split 3 ways = €100–180 each)

🎒Packing List

Layer everything. Iceland weather changes every hour. Forget the umbrella — it's useless in wind.

🧥 Clothing

  • Waterproof outer shell jacket
  • Fleece or down mid-layer
  • Merino wool base layers
  • Waterproof pants
  • Warm hat & buff/neck gaiter
  • Gloves (windproof)
  • Shorts & t-shirt (sunny days exist!)
  • Swimsuit (hot springs!)

👟 Footwear

  • Waterproof hiking boots (broken in!)
  • Casual shoes for Reykjavik
  • Wool socks (3-4 pairs)

📷 Gear

  • Camera + extra batteries (cold drains them)
  • Power bank
  • Travel adapter (Type C, Europlug — same as Germany ✓)
  • Sunglasses
  • Reusable water bottle (tap water is pristine)

📱 Essentials

  • Passport / ID
  • Credit card with PIN
  • Tourist SIM or roaming plan
  • Offline Google Maps downloaded
  • Booking confirmations (printed backup)
  • Sunscreen SPF30+ (yes, even in Iceland)

🏨Where to Stay

Night 1 — Reykjavik (Aug 24)

Stay downtown near Laugavegur for walkability. Guesthouses or Airbnb work great for 3 people.

  • Budget: KEX Hostel, Bus Hostel — from €40/person
  • Mid: Airbnb apartment (split 3 ways) — €50–80/person
  • Premium: Hotel Borg, Canopy by Hilton — from €120/person

Night 2 — Selfoss / Hella area (Aug 25)

After Golden Circle, base yourself in the south for early start next day.

  • Guesthouse Bitra B&B — cozy, affordable
  • Hotel Rangá — premium, great for northern lights spotting
  • Airbnb cottages near Hella — great value for groups

Night 3 — Vík / Kirkjubæjarklaustur (Aug 26)

Small towns — book early, limited options in August.

  • Puffin Hotel Vík — budget-friendly
  • Hotel Klaustur — reliable mid-range
  • Guesthouses and farm stays along Route 1

Night 4 — Höfn (Aug 27)

Gateway to Vatnajökull. Famous for langoustine restaurants.

  • Milk Factory — converted dairy, boutique vibe
  • Hotel Höfn — solid mid-range, glacier views
  • 🦞 Dinner at Pakkhús — best langoustine in Iceland
💡 Book NOW for August. Iceland accommodation fills up months in advance for peak summer. Airbnb is often the best value for 3 people — look for 2-bedroom apartments or cottages.
