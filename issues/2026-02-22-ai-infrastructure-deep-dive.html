Conclusion

AI infrastructure represents a multi-trillion dollar investment opportunity with structural growth tailwinds through 2030. The convergence of AI compute demand, power constraints, and supply chain bottlenecks creates a seller's market with exceptional pricing power for incumbent players.

The investment case rests on three pillars:

  1. Structural (not cyclical) demand: AI workloads require 10-100x more infrastructure than traditional cloud; enterprise adoption still in early innings
  2. Supply constraints protect returns: Power availability, CoWoS capacity, and HBM supply create barriers that limit new supply and protect incumbent margins
  3. Ecosystem moats compound: CUDA, hyperscaler relationships, and manufacturing scale create durable competitive advantages

Positioning: Overweight memory (SK Hynix, Micron) for best risk/reward; core positions in NVIDIA (platform dominance) and Vertiv (pure-play infrastructure); selective exposure to nuclear renaissance (Constellation, SMR developers) and data center REITs (Equinix, Digital Realty).

The 2026-2028 period will be critical for validating SMR deployment timelines, hyperscaler capex sustainability, and competitive dynamics. Monitor quarterly earnings, CoWoS capacity expansion, and power infrastructure developments closely.
