## AI & Machine Learning

### Google's TurboQuant Compression Sparks Memory Chip Sell-Off
- **Source:** CNBC, Google Research
- **URL:** https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/26/google-ai-turboquant-memory-chip-stocks-samsung-micron.html
- **Summary:** Google unveiled TurboQuant, a new compression method that can reduce AI model memory requirements by 6x with zero accuracy loss. The breakthrough sent memory chip stocks tumbling — SK Hynix fell 6%, Samsung nearly 5%, and Kioxia dropped 6% — as investors fear reduced demand for AI memory chips. Cloudflare CEO Matthew Prince called it "Google's DeepSeek," referencing last year's efficiency-driven sell-off. SemiAnalysis countered that improved efficiency typically leads to more capable models that ultimately require more hardware.

- **Source:** BleepingComputer
- **URL:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/european-commission-investigating-breach-after-amazon-cloud-account-hack/
- **Summary:** The European Commission is investigating a breach after a threat actor gained access to at least one of its AWS accounts. This is the Commission's second breach in 2026, following a January incident linked to Ivanti EPMM vulnerabilities that also hit the Dutch DPA and Finland's Valtori agency.

### New "Infinity Stealer" Malware Targets macOS via ClickFix
- **Source:** BleepingComputer, Malwarebytes
- **URL:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-infinity-stealer-malware-grabs-macos-data-via-clickfix-lures/
- **Summary:** A new info-stealing malware called Infinity Stealer targets macOS systems using ClickFix social engineering — fake Cloudflare CAPTCHA pages that trick users into pasting malicious commands in Terminal. The Python payload is compiled with Nuitka into a native macOS binary, making it significantly harder to detect and reverse-engineer than typical Python-based malware.

### Massive GitHub Campaign Uses Fake VS Code Alerts to Spread Malware
- **Source:** BleepingComputer, Socket
- **URL:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fake-vs-code-alerts-on-github-spread-malware-to-developers/
- **Summary:** A large-scale coordinated campaign is posting thousands of fake VS Code security alerts across GitHub Discussions, impersonating real maintainers with realistic CVE IDs. The posts link to malicious "patched" extensions hosted on Google Drive. Socket researchers found thousands of identical posts across repositories within minutes, triggering email notifications to massive numbers of developers.

### Backdoored Telnyx PyPI Package Delivers Malware via WAV Files

## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **Iran War Is the Dominant Story:** The Strait of Hormuz closure is driving oil toward $100+/barrel, reshaping inflation forecasts, and forcing central banks to contemplate rate hikes. The 1-3 week window before stopgap measures expire is the critical timeline. This is being called the worst oil shock since 1973.

2. **ECB Pivot — From Cuts to Hikes:** The ECB's hawkish turn is a dramatic reversal. With inflation forecasts ranging from 2.6% (base) to 4% (adverse), the rate-cutting cycle may be over. This has major implications for European equities, bonds, and the euro.

3. **AI Efficiency vs. AI Hardware Demand:** Google's TurboQuant represents a genuine efficiency breakthrough, but history (DeepSeek, Jevons paradox) suggests cheaper inference drives more usage. The memory chip sell-off may be overdone for long-term investors, but short-term volatility is real.

4. **Supply Chain Security Under Siege:** Between the Hormuz closure, Houthi attacks on shipping, EU cloud breaches, and developer-targeted supply chain attacks (PyPI, GitHub), both physical and digital supply chains are under extraordinary pressure.

5. **Political Dysfunction Compounds Risk:** The DHS shutdown with TSA agents missing paychecks, the contentious Fed Chair nomination, and G7 divisions all add layers of uncertainty at a time when coordinated policy responses are most needed.
