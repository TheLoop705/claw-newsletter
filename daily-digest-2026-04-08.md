## Semiconductors & Hardware

### Global Chip Sales Hit $88.8B in February — On Track for $1T Year
- **Source:** Semiconductor Industry Association
- **URL:** https://www.semiconductors.org/global-semiconductor-sales-increase-substantially-in-february/
- **Summary:** Global semiconductor sales reached $88.8 billion in February 2026, up 7.6% month-over-month and 61.8% year-over-year. The industry is on pace to reach approximately $975 billion in annual sales for 2026, with 26% projected growth. AI chips now drive roughly half of total revenue but represent less than 0.2% of total unit volume.
- **Impact:** The revenue-to-volume disconnect is striking — AI is creating a two-tier chip market where high-value silicon commands enormous premiums while non-AI buyers face rising costs and scarce inventory. This concentration risk is a key theme for semiconductor investors.

The company's 3nm supply remains scarce, and advanced packaging (CoWoS) faces immense demand. Nvidia is set to surpass Apple as TSMC's largest customer in 2026, reflecting the AI infrastructure buildout's dominance of leading-edge fab capacity.
- **Impact:** TSMC's capacity ceiling is becoming the binding constraint on AI infrastructure scaling. Companies without guaranteed TSMC allocations are exploring alternatives — including Intel Foundry Services. The upcoming April 16 earnings call will provide critical guidance on capacity expansion timelines and the impact of Middle East supply chain disruptions.

The timeline to meaningful Nvidia revenue displacement is still 2-3 years out.

- **Source:** The Hacker News / BleepingComputer

### LiteLLM Supply Chain Attack Hits Python Developers

- **URL:** https://www.cybersecurity-review.com/news-april-2026/

- **Impact:** AI developer tooling is becoming a prime target for supply chain attacks. The LiteLLM compromise is particularly concerning because it targets developers building AI applications — potentially providing access to API keys, model endpoints, and customer data. Pin your dependencies and verify checksums.

### Fortinet Critical Pre-Auth Bypass Under Active Exploitation
- **Source:** The Hacker News
- **URL:** https://thehackernews.com/
- **Summary:** Fortinet released emergency patches for CVE-2026-35616, a critical pre-authentication API access bypass in FortiClient EMS with a CVSS score of 9.1. The vulnerability has been exploited in the wild, allowing unauthenticated attackers to gain access to endpoint management systems.
- **Impact:** FortiClient EMS manages endpoint security policies — a compromise here gives attackers control over the security tooling itself. Patch immediately. Organizations using Fortinet products should audit for indicators of compromise and review EMS access logs for anomalous activity.
