# Newsletter Name Ideas

## Current: "Atoms Over Bits"

## Alternatives

### Directional/Trend-Focused
- **The Inflection Point** — capturing turning points in markets and technology
- **Asymmetric** — focusing on asymmetric risk/reward opportunities
- **The S-Curve** — adoption curves, growth phases, saturation points
- **Phase Change** — when systems shift states (solid/liquid/gas as metaphor)

### Research/Analysis Focused
- **Primary Source** — original research, not regurgitated
- **Due Diligence** — thorough, skeptical analysis
- **The Red Team** — challenging consensus views
- **Margin of Safety** — Graham/Buffett-inspired, risk-first thinking

### Time/Tempo Focused
- **Friday Briefing** — when it arrives, your routine
- **The Weekend Read** — for your Friday afternoon slot
- **Pre-Market** — before Monday opens
- **The 4:55** — end of week, before close

### Personality/Voice
- **Claw Research** — simple, your identity
- **Kimi's Notebook** — personal, informal
- **The Guest** — you're a visitor in their inbox
- **Second Opinion** — alternative to mainstream

## My Recommendation

**"The Inflection Point"** or **"Asymmetric"**

Both capture what you're doing:
- Finding moments where things change
- Looking for mispriced risk/reward
- Not following the herd

"The Inflection Point" is more descriptive.
"Asymmetric" is more punchy, memorable.

# Next Article Ideas (Publish Friday)

## Option 1: The Energy Transition Trade
**Thesis:** Everyone talks about renewables, but the real money is in the messy middle — grid infrastructure, storage, nuclear renaissance, and the companies enabling the transition without being pure-plays.

**Companies to research:**
- Vertiv (VRT) — data center power/cooling
- Quanta Services (PWR) — grid infrastructure
- Constellation Energy (CEG) — nuclear
- Cameco (CCJ) — uranium
- Eaton (ETN) — electrical components

**Angle:** Not greenwashing, actual infrastructure buildout.

## Option 2: The Great Labor Shortage
**Thesis:** Demographics + immigration constraints + early retirement = persistent labor shortages. Who benefits? Automation, robotics, workforce management software, vocational training.

**Companies to research:**
- Symbotic (SYM) — warehouse automation
- Intuit (INTU) — workforce management via Mailchimp/Credit Karma
- Workday (WDAY) — HR software
- Lincoln Electric (LECO) — welding equipment/training
- Copart (CPRT) — labor-light salvage operations

**Angle:** Structural, not cyclical. 10+ year trend.

---

## Option 3: The Small Cap Disconnect
**Thesis:** Large caps are crowded, expensive, and correlated. Small caps are ignored, cheap, and idiosyncratic. With rates potentially peaking, the valuation gap is extreme.

**Companies to research:**
- RBC Bearings (RBC) — precision components
- Heico (HEI) — aerospace parts
- Fair Isaac (FICO) — credit scores (not small but underappreciated)
- TransDigm (TDG) — aerospace components
- Several actually small caps TBD

**Angle:** Where the inefficiencies are. Less coverage = more alpha.

---

## Option 4: The Defense Renaissance
**Thesis:** Post-Ukraine, NATO spending commitments, US-China tension, and reshoring = multi-year defense capex cycle. Not just primes (Lockheed, RTX), but suppliers, cyber, drones, space.

**Companies to research:**
- Kratos (KTOS) — drones, hypersonics
- Mercury Systems (MRCY) — secure processing
- AeroVironment (AVAV) — tactical drones
- HEICO (HEI) — aerospace/defense components
- Palantir (PLTR) — defense software (controversial, but relevant)

**Angle:** The new Cold War is an industrial policy story.
