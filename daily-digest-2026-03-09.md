## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **Oil Shock Reshapes Everything:** Crude above $100 (and potentially headed to $150) is the dominant macro factor. This changes the calculus for inflation, interest rates, consumer spending, and virtually every sector. Energy and defense names outperform; airlines, retail, and growth stocks face headwinds.

2. **AI Spending Concentration Risk:** 83% of February's record $189B in VC went to just three companies. The AI capital arms race is accelerating but increasingly winner-take-all. Watch for downstream effects on smaller AI startups struggling to raise.

3. **Government-AI Tensions Escalating:** The US government designating Anthropic as a "supply chain risk" over military licensing disputes, combined with new "any lawful use" contract rules, signals a fundamental shift in the government-tech relationship. AI companies will face increasing pressure to comply with government use cases.

4. **Data Center Power Crisis Going Political:** The Ratepayer Protection Pledge is a political response to growing voter anger about electricity prices. With 86 GW of new capacity planned and Goldman Sachs forecasting 6% price increases, the energy-AI nexus is becoming a midterm election issue.

5. **Semiconductor Bifurcation Deepens:** China's push to create "China's ASML" while the US tightens chip export rules is accelerating the split of the global semiconductor supply chain. Anthropic's $21B Broadcom deal shows AI labs diversifying away from Nvidia dependency.

6. **Nvidia GTC as Next Catalyst:** With GTC starting March 16, Nvidia's announcements on Vera Rubin and Feynman architectures could provide a much-needed positive catalyst for battered tech stocks — or deepen the selloff if guidance disappoints amid the oil shock.
