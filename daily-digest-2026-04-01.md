## AI & Machine Learning

### Anthropic Signs AI Safety MOU with Australia
- **Source:** Reuters, Anthropic
- **URL:** https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/anthropic-sign-deal-with-australia-ai-safety-economic-data-tracking-2026-03-31/
- **Summary:** Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei met with Australian PM Anthony Albanese in Canberra to formalize a Memorandum of Understanding on AI safety. Under the agreement, Anthropic will share findings on emerging AI capabilities and risks, participate in joint safety evaluations, collaborate on research with Australian universities, and share its economic index data to help track AI adoption's impact on workers and jobs. The deal mirrors similar agreements Anthropic has signed in Japan and the UK.

### 55% of Americans Say AI Will Do More Harm Than Good
- **Source:** Bloomberg, LA Times, Quinnipiac University
- **URL:** https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2026-03-31/more-than-half-of-u-s-says-ai-is-likely-to-harm-them

The growing usage-distrust gap signals a maturing but anxious public relationship with AI.

## Tech Policy & Regulation

### California's CCPA Gets Teeth: AI Oversight and Risk Assessments Now Mandatory
- **Source:** Security Boulevard, Traverse Legal
- **URL:** https://securityboulevard.com/2026/03/california-gets-serious-about-regulation-again/
- **Summary:** California's updated CCPA/CPRA regulations, effective since January 1, 2026, are now being enforced in earnest. The new rules require formal risk assessments for high-risk data processing, oversight of automated decision-making technology (ADMT/AI), and independent cybersecurity audits. This represents the most aggressive state-level AI regulation in the US, with ADMT opt-out rules for consumers arriving January 1, 2027.

### California Executive Order Requires AI Safeguards for State Contracts

## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **War Premium Dominates Markets:** The Iran-Israel conflict has defined Q1 2026 — worst quarter since 2022, oil above $100, and defense stocks surging. The April 6 deadline for Hormuz reopening is the near-term catalyst that will determine whether markets recover or deteriorate further.

2. **AI Infrastructure ≠ AI Sentiment:** While Anthropic signs government deals and TSMC books out 2nm capacity through 2028, the public is increasingly skeptical — 55% of Americans say AI will harm them. This sentiment-investment disconnect could eventually influence regulation and policy.

3. **Energy-Tech Convergence Accelerates:** Microsoft's $7B Texas power deal with Chevron is the latest sign that Big Tech is becoming Big Energy. Companies that can deliver reliable power to data centers hold growing strategic value.

4. **Defense Tech Hits Escape Velocity:** Saronic's $1.75B raise at $9.25B valuation shows defense tech has graduated from niche to mainstream venture. The Hormuz crisis has provided real-world validation for autonomous naval platforms.

5. **Food Industry Consolidation:** McCormick-Unilever's ~$45B combination is the largest food deal in years, reflecting the sector's pursuit of scale amid cost pressures and shifting consumer preferences.

6. **Semiconductor Sovereignty Race:** China's 80% self-sufficiency target by 2030, TSMC's Japan expansion, and India's new chip facilities show the global chip supply chain is fragmenting into regional blocs.
