### Trump Waives Jones Act Shipping Rules for 60 Days to Stabilize Oil Market
- **Source:** CNBC
- **URL:** https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/18/trump-jones-act-oil-iran-war.html
- **Summary:** President Trump issued a 60-day waiver of the century-old Jones Act, allowing foreign vessels to transport oil, natural gas, fertilizer, and coal between U.S. ports. The move aims to stabilize domestic energy supply amid the Iran war. However, analysts note limited impact due to a mismatch between U.S. refinery capacity (built for Middle Eastern crude) and domestic shale oil production. ## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **Iran War Dominates Everything:** The escalation — Israel bombing South Pars, Iran retaliating against Qatar's Ras Laffan, Trump threatening to destroy Iranian gas fields — is the single biggest market driver. Energy infrastructure targeting has turned a regional conflict into a global economic event. Brent at $112 and rising.

2. **Stagflation Fears Crystallizing:** Hot PPI data + Fed holding rates + oil spike = classic stagflation setup. The Fed's one-cut projection looks optimistic if oil stays above $100. Markets are pricing 52% odds of zero cuts in 2026.

3. **Semiconductor Supply Chain Under Threat:** Qatar's helium supply (33% of global production) is at risk from ongoing attacks. This creates a non-obvious but critical chokepoint for chip manufacturing — potentially more disruptive than direct chip export controls.

4. **AI Investment Undeterred by Macro:** Micron's nearly 3x revenue growth, Tencent doubling AI spend, Nvidia launching NemoClaw, China's mass OpenClaw adoption — the AI capex cycle is accelerating regardless of geopolitical turmoil. But valuations are being tested by macro headwinds.

5. **Energy Security Is National Security:** Trump's Jones Act waiver, the Strait of Hormuz closure, and attacks on Gulf energy infrastructure are forcing a real-time reckoning on energy dependence. The U.S. produces plenty of oil but can't refine enough of its own crude for self-sufficiency.
