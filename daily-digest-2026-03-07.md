## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **Geopolitical Risk Repricing:** The Iran war is the dominant macro force—oil at $90+, Strait of Hormuz shut, and markets repricing everything from inflation to chip supply chains. The 35% weekly oil surge is the biggest since 1983. This is not a one-week story.

2. **AI's Government Collision:** The Anthropic-Pentagon standoff sets a historic precedent for AI safety vs. national security. The outcome will define whether AI companies can maintain ethical guardrails when selling to governments—or whether "any lawful use" becomes the standard.

3. **Capital Concentration at Extremes:** $156B of February's $189B VC record went to just three companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Waymo). This hyper-concentration is creating a bifurcated market where seed and early-stage funding is increasingly squeezed.

4. **Energy-AI Nexus Institutionalized:** The White House Ratepayer Pledge formalizes Big Tech's obligation to fund data center power infrastructure. With 86 GW of new capacity planned for 2026 and 100 GW of new data centers by 2030, energy is the binding constraint on AI growth.

5. **Semiconductor Supply at Risk:** The Iran conflict threatens chip materials, China is racing to build its own ASML, and Anthropic's $21B Broadcom commitment shows AI companies are bypassing traditional chip supply chains entirely. The semiconductor landscape is fracturing along geopolitical lines.
