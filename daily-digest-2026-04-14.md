## Cybersecurity

### Anthropic's Claude Mythos Discovers Thousands of Zero-Day Vulnerabilities
- **Source:** The Hacker News / NPR
- **URL:** https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/anthropics-claude-mythos-finds.html
- **Summary:** Anthropic's Claude Mythos Preview autonomously discovered and exploited zero-day vulnerabilities across every major operating system and web browser, including a 17-year-old RCE vulnerability in FreeBSD (CVE-2026-4747). In response, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing, restricting the model to ~50 select organizations and committing $100M in credits and $4M to open-source security.
- **Impact:** This is a watershed moment for AI-powered cybersecurity. The capability to autonomously find zero-days at this scale fundamentally changes the offense-defense balance. Partners include Amazon, Apple, Microsoft, CrowdStrike, and the Linux Foundation. The restricted-access model sets a precedent for how dangerous AI capabilities should be governed.

### Citrix NetScaler Zero-Day Actively Exploited (CVE-2026-3055)
- **Source:** DIESEC
- **URL:** https://diesec.com/2026/04/top-5-cybersecurity-news-stories-april-3-2026/
- **Summary:** Attackers are actively exploiting CVE-2026-3055 in Citrix NetScaler ADC/Gateway appliances to read sensitive data from appliance memory, enabling stolen session material and rapid follow-on access to corporate networks.
- **Impact:** Organizations running NetScaler should patch immediately. Session hijacking through memory disclosure is particularly dangerous because it bypasses MFA. This is a priority-one vulnerability for enterprise security teams.

### Chrome's Fourth Zero-Day of 2026 Patched (CVE-2026-5281)
- **Source:** BleepingComputer
- **URL:** https://www.bleepingcomputer.com
- **Summary:** Google released an out-of-band Chrome 146 update to fix CVE-2026-5281, marking the fourth actively exploited Chrome zero-day patched so far in 2026. Adobe also released emergency patches for Acrobat Reader to address vulnerabilities exploited in the wild since December.
- **Impact:** The pace of browser zero-days is accelerating — four in less than four months suggests either improved detection or a more aggressive threat landscape. Chrome's auto-update mechanism helps, but enterprise environments with delayed rollouts remain exposed.

### North Korean APT37 Launches Facebook-Based Social Engineering Campaign
- **Source:** The Hacker News
- **URL:** https://thehackernews.com/
- **Summary:** North Korean threat group APT37 has been attributed to a multi-stage social engineering campaign using Facebook friend requests as the initial attack vector. After building trust through social interaction, attackers deliver the RokRAT remote access trojan for persistent access.
- **Impact:** Social media platforms continue to be a blind spot in enterprise security. This campaign demonstrates that nation-state actors are willing to invest significant time in relationship-building before payload delivery, making detection extremely difficult.

## Tech Policy & Regulation

### Over 600 State AI Bills Introduced in 2026 Legislative Sessions
- **Source:** Inside Global Tech
- **URL:** https://www.insideglobaltech.com/2026/04/06/u-s-tech-legislative-regulatory-update-first-quarter-2026/
- **Summary:** State lawmakers across the U.S. have introduced more than 600 AI bills in 2026 sessions, creating a patchwork of regulations for private entities. Indiana, Utah, and Washington enacted laws prohibiting health insurers from using AI as the sole basis for denying claims.
- **Impact:** The sheer volume of state-level AI legislation creates compliance complexity that favors large enterprises with legal teams. Startups deploying AI nationally face a fragmented regulatory landscape. Health insurance AI restrictions signal broader sector-specific regulation ahead.

### DOJ Establishes AI Litigation Task Force to Challenge State Laws
- **Source:** Global Policy Watch
- **URL:** https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/04/u-s-tech-legislative-regulatory-update-first-quarter-2026/
- **Summary:** The Department of Justice created an AI Litigation Task Force in January 2026 with sole responsibility to challenge state AI laws that regulate interstate commerce or conflict with federal authority. This follows Trump's December 2025 AI Preemption Executive Order.
- **Impact:** Federal preemption of state AI laws could simplify compliance but also weaken consumer protections. The task force signals the administration views AI regulation as primarily a federal domain. Companies should prepare for potential invalidation of state-level requirements.

### NIST Launches AI Agent Standards Initiative
- **Source:** Global Policy Watch
- **URL:** https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/04/u-s-tech-legislative-regulatory-update-first-quarter-2026/
- **Summary:** NIST's Center for AI Standards and Innovation issued a Request for Information on agentic AI practices and launched the AI Agent Standards Initiative. The effort aims to develop industry standards for autonomous AI systems that can take actions and make decisions independently.
- **Impact:** Agentic AI standards will become critical as autonomous systems move from demos to production. Early engagement by companies building AI agents could shape standards in favorable directions. Expect these standards to become procurement requirements for government AI contracts.

### SELF DRIVE Act of 2026 Advances in Congress
- **Source:** Global Policy Watch
- **URL:** https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/04/u-s-tech-legislative-regulatory-update-first-quarter-2026/
- **Summary:** The House Energy and Commerce Committee held hearings on the SELF DRIVE Act of 2026 (H.R.7390), which would create a federal framework for autonomous vehicle deployment. The bill aims to preempt conflicting state regulations that have complicated AV rollouts.
- **Impact:** A federal AV framework would accelerate deployment by Waymo, Cruise, and others. The preemption angle mirrors the AI regulation approach — favoring national standards over state patchworks. This could unlock significant investment in AV fleet operations.
