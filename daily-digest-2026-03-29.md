## AI & Machine Learning

### Anthropic Sues Pentagon Over Supply-Chain Risk Designation
- **Source:** The Verge, Fortune
- **URL:** https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/891377/anthropic-dod-lawsuit
- **Summary:** Anthropic has sued the Department of Defense over its designation as a "supply-chain risk" — a label typically reserved for foreign cybersecurity threats, not US-headquartered companies. The suit accuses the Trump administration of illegal First Amendment retaliation for Anthropic's refusal to allow its AI to be used for mass domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons. A federal judge hearing the case noted that "punishing Anthropic is classic illegal First Amendment retaliation." A preliminary injunction decision is expected within days.

### SAG-AFTRA Bargains for "Tilly Tax" on AI Film Characters
- **Source:** Fortune, Bloomberg
- **URL:** https://fortune.com/2026/03/28/actors-union-sag-aftra-contract-bargaining-tilly-tax-ai-film-characters-hollywood-studios/
- **Summary:** The actors' union SAG-AFTRA is pushing for a "Tilly tax" — named after controversial AI actress Tilly Norwood — that would levy fees on AI-generated "synthetic" performers to make them cost as much as using real actors. Executive Director Duncan Crabtree-Ireland said collective bargaining has been "the fastest and most effective way for the regulation of AI technology." The union is also pushing Congress to pass the NO FAKES Act to give people ownership over their voice and likeness.

### Macy's AI Shopping Assistant Drives 400% More Spending

## Energy & Infrastructure

### Saudi East-West Pipeline Hits 7 Million Barrel/Day Capacity
- **Source:** Fortune, Bloomberg
- **URL:** https://fortune.com/2026/03/28/saudi-arabia-east-west-oil-pipeline-strait-hormuz-bypass-7-million-barrels-yanbu-red-sea/
- **Summary:** Saudi Arabia's crucial East-West pipeline — its contingency for the Strait of Hormuz closure — is now pumping at full capacity of 7 million barrels/day. Crude exports via the Red Sea port of Yanbu have reached ~5 million barrels/day, with additional refined products. However, the pipeline only partly offsets the 15 million barrels/day that previously flowed through Hormuz. The concern now shifts to whether Houthis will target Red Sea shipping, opening a new front.

### Meta Orders 10 Gas Power Plants for Hyperion AI Campus — 7.5GW Total
- **Source:** Fortune
- **URL:** https://fortune.com/2026/03/27/meta-hyperion-10-gas-power-plants-louisiana-entergy/
- **Summary:** Meta's deal with Entergy for 10 gas-fired power plants in Louisiana represents a 30%+ increase to the state's entire grid capacity. The 7.5GW of gas power plus 2.5GW of renewables will cost nearly $11 billion, all paid by Meta. Entergy stock jumped 7% on the news to a record $50B market cap. CEO Zuckerberg has said Hyperion will cover "a significant part of the footprint of Manhattan." Critics worry ratepayers could be stuck with costs after the 15-year contract expires.

### Grid Infrastructure Is the Real Problem, Not Data Centers
- **Source:** Fortune
- **URL:** https://fortune.com/2026/03/28/data-centers-grid-problem-infrastructure-ai/
- **Summary:** A new Senate bill targeting data center energy costs is being challenged by industry voices arguing the real problem is decades of underinvestment in grid infrastructure. Data centers now account for ~7% of US electricity demand (up from 1% fifteen years ago), but EVs, heat pumps, and industrial electrification are also straining a grid "built for a 20th-century world." A Duke University study found that curtailing just 0.25-1% of annual consumption during peak hours could absorb 100GW of new load without new generation.

## Investment Themes & Takeaways

1. **AI vs. Government — The Anthropic Precedent:** Anthropic's lawsuit against the Pentagon is the defining AI policy battle of the moment. The outcome will determine whether AI companies can set "red lines" on military use or whether the government can effectively blacklist them for doing so. The judge's comments suggest Anthropic has a strong case.

2. **Defense Tech Is the New Frontier:** Anduril ($60B), Shield AI ($1.5B), and the Army's drone marketplace collectively signal that defense tech has arrived as a first-tier venture category. The dual wars in Ukraine and Iran are accelerating adoption of autonomous systems at a pace that traditional defense contractors can't match.

3. **Energy Infrastructure — The Bottleneck:** Meta's 7.5GW power order, Saudi Arabia's pipeline maxing out, and the grid infrastructure debate all point to the same conclusion: energy infrastructure is the binding constraint for both AI growth and geopolitical stability.

4. **Consumer Inflation Broadening:** Sony's console price hikes, petrochemical cost pass-throughs, and retailer warnings suggest consumer-facing inflation is broadening beyond fuel. The ECB's hawkish posture and weak bond markets reflect this reality.

5. **Supply Chain Attacks Intensifying:** From the European Commission's AWS breach to FBI Director Patel's hack to PyPI/GitHub developer-targeted attacks, both nation-state and criminal actors are ramping up supply chain operations. The attack surface keeps expanding.
